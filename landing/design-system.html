Design system

The Purely Personal
design system.

How the brand looks, sounds, and feels. The colors, the type, the components, the voice. Built for surfaces that need to feel quiet, calm, and trustworthy.

Principles

Six rules, applied everywhere

The brand ships products that live on people's laptops with their most sensitive files. So the design language has to feel like a quiet tool, not a loud app. These are the six rules we hold every surface to.

1

Quiet confidence

No exclamation marks in body copy. No "revolutionary." No "unlock." Say the thing. Let it speak for itself.

2

Warm, not white

The base is cream, not pure white. Every surface feels paper-like. Reassuring. Low-blue. Easy on tired eyes.

3

Serif for human moments

Iowan Old Style for headings, quotes, the logo. San serif for everything functional. The serif reads like someone wrote it.

4

Coral as the only accent

One color does all the heavy lifting. No rainbow palettes. Coral means "attention here." Everything else is neutral.

5

Proof over claim

When we say "private," we show the WiFi-off demo. When we say "local," we cite the network monitor. Visuals back every promise.

6

No em dashes

Em dashes are the AI tell. A real person writes in commas or full stops. Every piece of copy runs through a de-em-dash pass.

Color

Cream, ink, and a single coral

Three primaries plus three supporting colors. Every surface uses a subset. Never all of them. Coral is the only brand color — everything else is neutral weight.

Typography

Serif for feeling. Sans for function.

A two-family stack. Iowan Old Style anchors every heading, the logo, and quotes. The body uses the OS system font so it always feels native. Nothing loaded from a CDN.

Iconography

Emoji, by design

We use native OS emoji for icons. No custom icon font. No SVG sets. Rationale: emoji ships with every device, renders consistently enough, and feels human. The icon stays the same whether you're on Mac, Windows, or Linux.

Voice

How it sounds

Short sentences. Direct. No hype. The reader should feel like a calm, smart friend is telling them what's actually going on, not a pitch deck.

Do this, not that

Do

Every professional has a folder of documents they'd never paste into ChatGPT. This is for those.

Don't

Unlock next-gen productivity by leveraging revolutionary on-device AI — transforming the way you work forever!

Do

It's a 4.7 GB file on your disk. Nothing magic about it. Open source, read the code.

Don't

Our cutting-edge proprietary neural architecture empowers you to achieve results previously impossible.

Do

Turn off WiFi. Ask a question. It still works. That's the proof.

Don't

Experience unparalleled data sovereignty with our end-to-end privacy-first paradigm shift.

Words we use

On your laptop. Private. Local. Free. Open source. Offline. Yours. Straightforward. Real. Honest. Works. Fixes. Solves.

Words we never use

Never. Leverage. Unlock. Synergy. Revolutionary. Paradigm. Supercharge. Empower. Transform. Cutting-edge. Seamless. Magical. Disruptive. Bespoke. Curated.

Punctuation rules

  • No em dashes. Use a comma or a period. Em dashes are the AI tell.
  • No exclamation marks in body copy. Save them for demos.
  • Oxford comma: on.
  • Sentence case in UI. Title Case in marketing heads only.
  • Short paragraphs. Two to three sentences per block, max.

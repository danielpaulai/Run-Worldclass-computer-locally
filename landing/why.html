Common objections (answered)

My laptop isn't a supercomputer. Can it really run AI?

Yes. The model we use, qwen2.5:7b, needs about 5 GB of RAM. It's the best model in its class as of early 2026. On a 2023+ MacBook it feels as fast as ChatGPT. On an 8 GB machine it's slower on the first message but still usable.

We pick the model to match your laptop. You won't be sitting watching a progress bar.

Is this worse than ChatGPT?

For the things it's good at, it's comparable. For summarization, second drafts, explaining concepts, quick brainstorming, and code review on files under 5,000 lines, you won't notice the difference in quality for most tasks. For hard reasoning problems, cutting-edge knowledge, or very long documents, ChatGPT and Claude are meaningfully better.

The session teaches you the line between the two.

Is "local" actually local? How do I know?

Three ways: (1) The whole system is open source, you can read every line. (2) You can turn off your WiFi during the session and the AI keeps working. (3) Your OS shows you what's connecting where, we'll look at it live.

This isn't a marketing claim. It's observable.

What's the catch?

Three honest ones:

1. Capability ceiling. A 7B model on a laptop won't beat a 400B model in the cloud on hard problems. You'll know when you hit the ceiling, and that's fine, you still have ChatGPT for those.

2. Setup is one-time friction. 15 minutes tonight. After that, it's normal.

3. The model doesn't know the last few months. It's trained on data up to a date. If you need last-week's news, you need a search tool or a cloud AI.

Why free? Who pays for this?

Nobody. Ollama is open source (Apache 2). Qwen 2.5 is open-weights (Alibaba released it). Purely Personal (this app) is MIT-licensed. I built it on my own time because I think everyone should have private AI.

No subscription is coming. No "pro tier." The whole thing stays free.

I already use ChatGPT. Why do I need this?

For the documents you currently don't paste into ChatGPT. Think about the last time you hesitated. That hesitation is the wedge this session is for.

Also, once you own the stack, you're not paying a rent for AI. You're running it.

# Example: `local-tutor feynman "transformer"`

**Model:** `qwen2.5:7b`
**Prompt:** *Explain a transformer neural network using the Feynman technique. Use only everyday language a 10-year-old would understand. Use an analogy. Max 5 sentences.*

## What came out

> Imagine your brain is like a big toy box full of different toys. A transformer is like a magical helper that can take any toy, understand it really well, and then use its magic to make new, cool toys or change old ones in amazing ways!
>
> Now think about when you're playing with your friends. Sometimes one friend takes a toy and shares it with others by telling exactly how to build it. Transformers do something similar but with words instead of toys.
>
> In the world of computers, transformers help understand words like your friends sharing toys, then they can change or create new sentences just like making up fun games to play!
>
> So, transformers are like smart magical friends that help computers understand and make language super fun and useful!

## Teacher's note

**Quality: OK.** The model pitched it well for the audience (10-year-old language, toys analogy, enthusiastic tone). It hit the right tone, but the analogy is fuzzy — it doesn't really explain *what* a transformer does mechanically (attention, parallel processing) because it never picked a precise analogy.

**Use this as a comparison demo:** Ask the same prompt of cloud Claude next to this. Students will see cloud's sharper analogy side by side with local's friendlier-but-fluffier one. Good for building judgment.

**Time:** ~10 seconds.
